Safety & Health

Protecting our Workforce

Our safety and health professionals play an important role in providing expertise and support to our operations throughout the Permian Basin. Our employees are engaged in identifying and managing risk and are active participants in continuously improving the health and safety of our company. Our senior leaders also share a deep commitment to safety and health as core values and consistently and clearly communicate Concho’s expectations throughout the ranks of the organization. Examples of such commitment are evidenced in our Safety Management System and the fact that employee safety and health is factored into our executive overall compensation.

Concho’s Safety Management System defines the fundamental safety and health principles by which we run our business. Our Safety Management System consists of five interrelated components:

1

Compliance, Injury and Illness Prevention Programs

Developing and implementing programs based on OSHA guidelines. This helps ensure that we comply with applicable Federal and State regulations focused on employee and contractor safety. Our programs include:

Stop Work Program

Every Concho employee and contractor is empowered to call for a halt to work when unsafe conditions are observed, in order to prevent incidents.

Incident Investigation Process

Specially trained personnel use a methodical and systematic approach to investigate all incidents. This process includes developing and verifying the implementation of effective corrective measures.

Near Miss Reporting and Analysis

Near miss incidents are reported and investigated based on potential severity, using the same methodology used in incidents resulting in loss.

American Exploration and Production Council (AXPC) Benchmarking

Our participation in AXPC allows Concho to benchmark recordable injury incident rates against industry peers to determine if current rates are within normalized ranges for the industry.

Protecting Workers

We conduct atmospheric and noise sampling at appropriate intervals to ensure proper regulations are followed to protect employees and contract workers on Concho locations.

2

Leadership

Facilitating effective safety and health leadership throughout Concho. This includes annual safety leadership courses developed to educate supervisors to drive safe work practices. These programs are coupled with highly visible field presence from health and safety personnel conducting safety compliance reviews for contractors and Concho employees.

3

Improvement

Employing a continuous quality improvement model to analyze and optimize our operations. Our model is based on investigations of both leading and lagging indicators, with a heavy emphasis on leading indicators such as Stop Work, Near Misses, Contractor Feedback and Hazard Observations.

4

Training

Providing ongoing training to employees about safe work practices, regulatory guidelines, process improvements and lessons learned during incident reviews. Concho requires 100% in-person attendance each month for field training. In 2016, all Concho field supervisors completed a Safety and Environmental Leadership course; in 2017, they completed an Incident Response, Investigation and Reporting course.

5

Contractor Accountability

Holding contract companies accountable for compliance with Concho’s expectations for providing a safe work environment. Our health and safety team conducts field compliance reviews to promote safe and compliant work practices by contractors. We also monitor contractors’ insurance, incident rates, safety policies and training programs using the widely accepted ISNetworld.

Comprehensive Emergency Action Plan

Thorough preparation is the best way to respond to any emergency. That’s why we have developed comprehensive plans for training all Concho employees. Our plan includes:

Significant Incidents

Employees know how to respond to incidents that require immediate attention, such as a serious injury.

Releases

We have developed a comprehensive Emergency Action Plan for effective notification and management of environmental releases. We also review and update our Spill Prevention Control and Countermeasure plans on a regular basis as required by the EPA.

CONCHO
